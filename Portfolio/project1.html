UK Cinema

UX & UI case study - Group Project - Spring 2023 - The Hague Unviersity of Applied Sciences

Problem statement

UK cinema needs an effective digital strategy to successfully adapt to the mature Dutch market. The challenge is to create an online platform that responds to Dutch customers' desire for a sense of community while providing the best possible user experience.

The Goal

We aimed to create a unique, personalized digital platform that meets the expectations of Dutch consumers while remaining true to the brand identity of UK Cinema. The brand-new digital platform should also increase the number of users of the membership program. To achieve that, we used several UX methods and mapping.

User Research

UK Cinema’s target audience consists of people aged between 16-26 y.o. Following desk research, we uncovered that Dutch users prefer websites that offer loyalty programs, discounts, and other incentives to encourage them to return. Additionally, they have a strong sense of community and enjoy sharing their opinions and reviews of movies. User needs were converted into user stories, which were prioritized by using the MosCow method based on the findings from the research. As a result, the UX process was tailored to meet the needs of the target audience and to effectively promote community engagement.

User Flow

To identify user mental models, we used the card sorting method
which provided us with valuable insights.

The insights helped us create an effective Information Architecture for the website.
